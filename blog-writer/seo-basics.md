# SEO Basics

Lightweight rules. These cover 80% of what matters for a blog post.

---

## Title

- Include the primary keyword naturally. Do not stuff it.
- 50 to 60 characters. Longer titles get truncated in search results.
- Front-load the keyword when possible. "Blog Writing Skills: What Claude Code Taught Me" beats "What Claude Code Taught Me About Blog Writing Skills."
- Use a number if the post is a list. Numbers get clicks. "5 Things" beats "Several Things."

## Meta Description

- One to two sentences. Under 155 characters.
- Include the primary keyword once.
- Tell the reader what they will get, not what the post is about. "How to write blog posts that don't sound like AI wrote them" beats "A discussion of AI writing patterns."
- Do not end with a period if it makes the description feel cut off.

## Headings (H2, H3)

- Use H2 for major sections. H3 for subsections within those.
- Do not use headings just to break up text. Use them when a reader scanning the page needs a landmark.
- Include a keyword in at least one H2 if it fits naturally. Do not force it.

## Body

- First 100 words matter most to search engines. Get the primary topic established early.
- Link to one or two relevant internal posts if they exist. Do not link out to competitors.
- Images get alt text that describes the image and includes the keyword if natural.

## URL Slug

- Lowercase, hyphens between words. No underscores, no special characters.
- Short. Three to five words. "blog-writing-ai-skills" not "how-to-write-a-blog-post-using-ai-writing-skills-for-beginners."

---

## What Not to Chase

- Keyword density percentages. Write naturally. Stuffing hurts more than it helps.
- Exact-match anchor text every time. Vary it.
- Meta keywords. Ignored by every major search engine. Skip it.
