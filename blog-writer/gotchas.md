# Gotchas Reference

This file grows over time. Every time a post comes back with a problem, add one line here.
Short, specific, earned from a real failure. That is all.

---

## Discovered Patterns

- **The restatement opener.** Post opens with a sentence that just restates the title in different words. Delete it.
- **The buried lede.** The most interesting sentence in the post is in paragraph three. Move it to sentence one.
- **Passive voice creep.** "It has been shown that..." becomes "X showed that..." or cut entirely.
- **Hedging chains.** "This might possibly suggest that in some cases..." Pick a lane. Say the thing or cut it.
- **Transition word addiction.** "Furthermore", "moreover", "additionally" at the start of paragraphs. Replace with a stronger first clause or restructure.
- **The non-ending ending.** Post trails off rather than landing. If the last paragraph could be deleted without anyone noticing, rewrite it or delete it and let the second-to-last paragraph close.
- **Audience drift.** Post starts writing for one audience and shifts to another mid-way. Pick one reader and write to them the whole way through.
- **Subheading dependency.** Post relies on H2 subheadings to carry structure instead of letting paragraphs do it. If the subheadings were removed, would the post still make sense? It should.

---

## How to Add a Gotcha

When a post comes back with a problem you did not anticipate, add one line:

```
- **[Short name].** [One sentence describing the failure and the fix.]
```

Keep it under two sentences. If it needs more explanation than that, it is probably two gotchas.
